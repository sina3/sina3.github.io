About Me

TLDR:

I am Sina Rezazadeh. I live in Hamilton, Ontario, Canada. I was a mechanical engineer for a long time. Pandemic put my career on a pause and I decided to shift to programming. It was simple! I just needed to teach myself iOS development. So, I just did that. I published an app to App Store and have more coming. Contact me via email, Twitter or LinkedIn.

Know me more:

After many years of studying and professional experience in mechanical engineering and mechatronics and taking roles such as Project Engineer and Mechatronic Designer, pandemic caused a hiccup in my career. I paused for a while and pondered my options for the next move. Although I liked my mechanical engineering roles, deep down I wanted to feel more creative and in touch with tech.

Like many crossroads in my career, coding was a very tempting choice. I had always felt inclined towards programming and algorithmic thinking in general, but I mainly focused on jobs related to my education. I decided to take a leap of faith and start with this ambition. My journey began by learning basics of programming and computer science and then focused on iOS development. I was thrilled. I had never felt like that about what I was doing. So, I kept on learning more.

I used various resources available online, most significantly Stanford course CS193p "Developing Apps for iOS" taught by Paul Hegarty. This course helped me grasp a foundational understanding of iOS SDK including Swift, XCode and UIKit. When I did enough studying and finished many tutorial projects, it was time to start my own projects. I started simple and wrote my first app, a game called 3MM and could successfully publish it on App Store. This was a milestone for me and gave me extra confidence that I can make it as a developer.

Currently, I am working on multiple projects consolidating my skills in iOS development. I am also actively looking for iOS developer position in Canada or remote elsewhere. At the same time, I am continuously learning many subjects such as persistence with CoreData, testing and XCTest, ARKit, CI/CD systems and more. I also plan extend my UI development skills and develop some of my future projects with SwiftUI.

I know I have a lot to learn but I am ready to start as a junior iOS developer. You can check my resume and projects if you are interested. If you want to hire me or know about a position that fits my profile, please contact me via email. You can also connect with me on Twitter or LinkedIn.

When I'm not working, I spend time with my wife on many shared hobbies that we have. I enjoy hiking, camping, fishing, kayaking, exploring nature, playing tennis, and getting together with old and new friends on the weekends. We also used to travel when there wasn’t a never-ending pandemic.
